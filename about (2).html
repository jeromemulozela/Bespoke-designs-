Who We Are

A Different Kind
of Design Firm

Bespoke Designs Zambia was born from a simple frustration: too many beautiful visions lost to poor craftsmanship, missed deadlines, and broken promises from contractors who disappear once the contract is signed.

We built something different. A firm where every project — regardless of size — receives the full attention of experienced designers and builders who take genuine pride in the outcome.

Founded in Lusaka and working across Zambia, we bring international design sensibility to every space. From single-room renovations to complete residential builds, our process is defined by transparency, precision, and a relentless commitment to quality.

Seven years and over 85 projects later, our reputation is built not on marketing — but on the spaces we leave behind and the clients who return.

What We Stand For

Principles We Never Compromise

These aren't slogans — they are the standards by which every decision at Bespoke Designs Zambia is made.

Uncompromising Quality

We source premium materials and work only with verified, skilled artisans. Quality is not a feature we offer — it is the baseline we refuse to drop below.

Radical Transparency

You will always know where your project stands. Clear timelines, honest cost breakdowns, and proactive communication — from first call to final handover.

Client-Centred Design

Your vision drives every decision. We listen before we design, and we collaborate throughout — because the best outcome is always the one you actually wanted.

Local Pride, Global Standard

We are proudly Zambian. We employ Zambian talent, support local suppliers where possible, and apply international design and construction standards to every project.

Promises Kept

Our 100% on-time delivery record is not a coincidence — it is the result of rigorous planning, honest scheduling, and a team that does not make commitments it cannot keep.

Built to Last

We design and build for longevity. Every material, joint, and finish is selected not just for how it looks today — but for how it holds up in ten years' time.

Design Is
